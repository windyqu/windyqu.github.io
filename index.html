Tianyao Qu

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Sociology at Cornell University. My research areas are centered broadly in the areas of social networks, health, aging and the life course, economic sociology, and gender. Within these areas, my current work uses quantitative, social network analysis, sequence analysis, and mixed methods to understand the structural dynamics of both physical and digitalized social relationships and their consequences on health and well-being across the life course and over time. My research has been funded by the Cornell Center of Economy & Society, Cornell Center for the Study of Inequality, and Cornell Center for Social Sciences Qualitative & Interpretive Research Institute.

Research Interests

Social Networks, Health, Aging and the Life Course, Economic Sociology, Communication Technology, Gender and Sexuality
